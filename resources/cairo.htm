Data Transformation and Visualization

Background:

Most datasets have a certain location aspect, either a physical address or an IP address that identify a location or may be coordinates directly. Mining location data require in most cases an integration of additional supportive dataset and in most cases visualizing. Visualizing location data is an art.


Objective

Transforming Text-based origin destination matrix (OD) with the support of additional open source geospatial data to extract new insights that was impossible to retrieve from the original data.

Original Text-based OD matrix contain trip starting zone, arrival zone, departure time and arrival time. We transform each trip to Spatio-Temporal locations called people flow using Openstreet maps and Costumed land use map to be able to mine more details. Details are as flows:

(4) Data mining for new insights

 

This transformation results in high temporal resolution mobility which can give very important information. Most importantly, The Spatio-Temporal distribution of the population in the city. This means, now we not only can answer [Who is where and when?] but [How many people are expected to be in a specific place in a specific time of the day] example [how many people are expected to be at Odaiba at 3 pm on a normal weekday? This is priceless information for outdoor advertisement and traffic control
